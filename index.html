About

01

I came to USC with a suitcase and a half-formed theory about why software systems break at scale.

Three years in, the theory is sharper. I've spent time understanding how operating systems manage memory, how databases maintain consistency under concurrent load, and how networks fail — then wrote software that tries to work despite all of that.

Outside of code: I read philosophy of science, run long distances somewhat competitively, and believe deeply that a well-commented codebase is a form of respect for the next person.

I'm originally from China, which means I've had the strange privilege of learning to think in two languages and two programming paradigms simultaneously.
