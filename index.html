About

01

I came up through three universities on two continents — SJTU, Michigan, and now USC — and that circuitous path turned out to be a feature, not a bug.

My research sits at the intersection of machine learning and systems: making models more reliable, more private, and easier to debug. At OrderLab (UMich), I built TRAINCHECK — a framework that catches silent training bugs via inferred invariants, published at OSDI 2025. At USC I work on differential privacy for medical vision-language models, and at the HAT Lab on game-theoretic AI with graph networks and VAEs.

I care about measurement. The best ML work I've done came from being obsessive about evaluation metrics, ablations, and failure modes — not just the headline number.

Training with Confidence: Catching Silent Errors in Deep Learning Training with Automated Proactive Checks

Jiang, Yuxuan · Zhou, Ziming · Xu, Boyu · Liu, Beijie · Xu, Runhui · Huang, Peng

19th USENIX Conference on Operating Systems Design and Implementation (OSDI '25) — Boston, MA, July 2025

TRAINCHECK is a proactive framework that detects silent ML training bugs by automatically inferring training invariants and checking them at runtime — without requiring bug-specific oracles. Catches 18/20 real-world bugs, uncovers 6 previously unknown ones (3 confirmed upstream), and achieves >9× higher detection coverage than anomaly-based baselines with minimal runtime overhead.
